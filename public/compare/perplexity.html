Key Differences Explained

üéØ Purpose: Research vs. Verdict

Perplexity helps you explore topics and gather information. Verity gives you a definitive answer: Is this claim true or false?

  • Perplexity: "Here's what I found about this topic..."
  • Verity: "This claim is FALSE. Here's why, here are the sources."
  • For journalism, you need verdicts, not more information to sort through

üî¢ Confidence Scores Matter

Verity doesn't just tell you something is true or false‚Äîit tells you how confident the system is, and why.

  • 98% confidence = All 15 models agree, strong source consensus
  • 75% confidence = Most models agree, some conflicting sources
  • Below 60% = Flagged as "needs human review"
  • Perplexity provides no such transparency

  • Provides the "jury" approach to fact-checking
